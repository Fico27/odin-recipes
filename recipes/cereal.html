Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 cups coarse bran
  • 4 tablespoons ground flax
  • 4 tablespoons molasses (maple syrup, brown sugar or honey will also work)
  • 2 tablespoons avocado oil (or any other flavorless oil)
  • 2/3 cup water
  • Optional add-ins: 1 cup raisins, 1/2 cup dried fruit, or 1/2 cup nuts or seeds

Steps

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, coarse bran, ground flax, and salt. Mix well to evenly distribute the ingredients.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients: In a separate microwave-safe bowl or a small saucepan, combine the molasses (or your chosen sweetener), avocado oil, and water. Warm the mixture gently until it’s easy to stir and the ingredients are well combined. You want it to be warm, not hot.
  4. Mix and Knead: Pour the warm molasses mixture over the dry ingredients. Stir until everything is well combined. The dough should come together into a ball. If it’s too dry, add a little more water, one tablespoon at a time, until it reaches the desired consistency. If it’s too wet, add a touch more flour.
  5. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the preheated 300°F (150°C) and bake for 40-50 minutes or until the cereal becomes golden brown and crisp. Be sure to check and stir the cereal every 10 minutes to ensure even baking and prevent burning. You’ll want use a fork to occasionally stir through to prevent clumping.
  6. Cool and Break Apart: Allow the baked cereal to cool completely on the baking sheet. Once cooled, break it apart into small pieces or chunks. You can use your hands or a spoon to do this.
  7. Serve: Enjoy your homemade Grape Nuts cereal with your choice of milk (dairy or non-dairy), yogurt, or as a topping for smoothie bowls. You can also mix it with fresh fruit for a delightful breakfast or snack.
